# Inaugural International Orbitrap-IRMS Users Meeting
5–6 July 2025 <br/>
Prague, Czech Republic <br/>
  
<ins>Convenors</ins>: Amy Hofmann (Caltech), Issaku Kohl(University of Utah), Andrea Erhardt (University of Kentucky)<br/>
  
<ins>Description</ins>:<br/>
The use of Orbitrap-based mass spectrometry for isotopic analysis ('Orbitrap-IRMS') has grown significantly over the past ~10 years, and we expect that it will only continue to grow. This workshop is meant to initialize the development of a more formal Community of Practice among Orbitrap-IRMS users (and those interested in adopting the technology). We thus seek participation from individuals at all career stages and particularly encourage early- and early-mid-career scientists to attend.  

This two-day workshop will consist of a brief introduction to Orbitrap-based mass spectrometry for isotopic analysis, followed by oral presentations and posters interleaved with moderated discussion sessions. These sessions will focus primarily on scientific results but will also include topics like new analytical methods, interlaboratory comparisons (including standard ring tests and calibrations), data processing, etc. The workshop will culminate with a discussion led by the organizers at the end of the second day. In this ‘synthesis discussion’, we will revisit and summarize the preceding two days’ events, solicit feedback for improvement and suggestions for next year’s follow-on meeting, identify community ‘needs’ and ‘wants’ (possibly leading to spin-off working groups), and select 1–2 new co-convenors who will work with one of us to organize and plan next year’s meeting. We anticipate that this workshop will lead to new collaborations among not only the current Orbitrap-IRMS users but also those interested in entering the field. Ultimately, we aim to initiate a self-perpetuating, community-driven meeting that recurs annually for the first few years before transitioning to a bi-annual cadence akin to the International (Carbonate) Clumped Isotope Workshop (now in its 9th iteration).  

Those who wish to present original research are encouraged to bring a poster. We welcome contributions that present scientific results, scientific works-in-progress, novel methodological approaches and/or instrument adaptations, and "lessons learned" that touch on these or any other related Orbitrap-IRMS-based investigation.  

The workshop will be led by Dr. Amy Hofmann (Caltech), Dr. Issaku Kohl (University of Utah), and Dr. Andrea Erhardt (University of Kentucky) with several invited presentations.  

Lunch is included on both days.
